This is where most of America actually lives. These 159 counties cross the Census Bureau's historical density line for "urban" — 1,000 people per square mile. Together, they hold nearly 40% of the country's population on well under 2% of its land.

Compare it with the other end of the map — 46% of the land holding barely 1% of the people. See all three density cutoffs side by side.
