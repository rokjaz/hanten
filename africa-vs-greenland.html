Only two African countries are bigger than Greenland

Greenland looks continent-sized on the Mercator map. At true, equal-area scale, only Algeria and DR Congo actually beat it — and the next two largest African countries, Sudan and Libya, fall short.

Algeria and DR Congo barely clear the bar. Both are within about 10% of Greenland's true land area — drawn at equal-area scale, each nearly traces Greenland's own outline. Sudan and Libya, the third- and fourth-largest countries on the continent, fall meaningfully short: about 14% and 19% smaller.

This is the flip side of the 14-Greenlands map: Greenland only looks continent-sized because of the Mercator projection. At true scale it's closer in size to one large country than to Africa itself — it just happens to be a country-sized landmass that almost no African country can beat.

Areas: CIA World Factbook. Country shapes: Natural Earth. Each shape above uses its own equal-area projection centered on itself, all at identical scale — the same method used across our True Size of Africa maps.
