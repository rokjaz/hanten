An uninhabited island group with three names and three claimants

Under 3 square miles of rock, currently administered by Japan — and formally claimed by both China and Taiwan, each under its own name. This is a naming dispute layered on top of a live territorial one.

🇯🇵 Administered by Japan

Senkaku Islands

Administered by Japan since 1972, as part of Ishigaki City, Okinawa Prefecture. Japan's position is that no sovereignty dispute exists to negotiate.

🇨🇳 Claimed by China

Diaoyu Islands

China cites historical records referencing the islands dating back centuries, and maintains its own patrols in nearby waters to assert the claim.

🇹🇼 Claimed by Taiwan

Diaoyutai Islands

Taiwan makes a historical claim similar to China's, administered on paper as part of Yilan County — a separate claim from, not coordinated with, Beijing's.

Nobody lives there, but the waters around it matter. The islands themselves are barren and uninhabited, but the surrounding waters hold fishing grounds and potential undersea gas reserves, and sit near busy shipping lanes. Japanese coast guard vessels routinely intercept Chinese and Taiwanese boats entering waters Japan considers its own — incidents that periodically escalate into diplomatic standoffs between the three governments.
