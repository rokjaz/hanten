The Lower 48 is almost exactly 4 Greenlands

On the Mercator world map, Greenland looks close to continent-sized. Here it is at true, equal-area scale, laid over the contiguous United States.

The contiguous United States is about 3.7 Greenlands. At true scale: 8.08M km² for the Lower 48 versus 2.17M km² for Greenland. Mercator inflates anything far from the equator, and Greenland — sitting almost entirely above 60°N — is one of its biggest exaggerations.

Each shape here is Greenland, redrawn with an equal-area projection at exactly the same scale as the US map — rotated freely, since only the area is the claim, not the orientation. Three of them span the country side to side; the fourth exists only because 3.7 isn't a whole number, and it shows — wherever a Greenland spills past the coastline, an equal patch of the country goes uncovered somewhere else. The Pacific Northwest, New England, and Florida all peek through untouched.

Areas: CIA World Factbook (contiguous US ≈ 8.08M km², Greenland ≈ 2.17M km², ratio ≈ 3.7). Shapes: Natural Earth / US Census state boundaries. Every Greenland uses its own azimuthal equal-area projection at the US map's scale, so all shapes here are directly comparable.
